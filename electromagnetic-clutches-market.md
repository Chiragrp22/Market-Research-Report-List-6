<p><p>Electromagnetic clutches are classified into several types based on their operating conditions and materials. Dry clutches operate in an air-filled environment, providing efficient engagement and disengagement without fluid lubrication. Wet clutches, on the other hand, are submerged in lubricant, reducing friction and wear while allowing higher torque transmission. Magnetic powder clutches utilize a magnetic field to control the engagement of particles, enabling smooth operation and torque regulation. Other types may include various hybrid systems or specialized designs serving specific applications.</p></p>
