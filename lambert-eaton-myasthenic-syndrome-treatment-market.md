<p><p>Lambert-Eaton Myasthenic Syndrome (LEMS) treatment options include several key categories. Potassium channel blockers, like Amifampridine, enhance neuromuscular transmission by blocking potassium channels. Cholinesterase inhibitors, such as Pyridostigmine, increase acetylcholine availability at the neuromuscular junction. Immune therapies aim to modulate the immune response, while intravenous immunoglobulin provides passive immunity, helping to reduce symptoms. Plasmapheresis is a procedure that removes harmful antibodies from the blood, offering another approach to alleviate symptoms and improve muscle strength in patients.</p></p>
