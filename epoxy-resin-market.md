<p><ul><li>DGBEA (Bisphenol A and ECH)</li><li>DGBEF (Bisphenol F and ECH)</li><li>Novolac (Formaldehyde and Phenols)</li><li>Aliphatic (Aliphatic Alcohols)</li><li>Glycidylamine (Aromatic Amines and ECH)</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The epoxy resin market encompasses various types, each distinguished by their chemical compositions. DGBEA combines Bisphenol A with epichlorohydrin (ECH), offering good mechanical properties. DGBEF uses Bisphenol F and ECH, providing better heat resistance. Novolac resins, derived from formaldehyde and phenols, are known for high thermal stability. Aliphatic epoxies utilize aliphatic alcohols for flexible applications. Glycidylamine resins, synthesized from aromatic amines and ECH, provide enhanced toughness. Other types include specialty formulations catering to specific industrial needs.</p></p>
