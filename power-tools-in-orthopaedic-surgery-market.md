<p><ul><li>Electric Powered</li><li>Battery Operated</li><li>Pneumatic Powered</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Power tools used in orthopaedic surgery come in three main types: electric powered, battery operated, and pneumatic powered. Electric powered tools are commonly used in operating rooms and provide consistent power for precision cutting and drilling. Battery operated tools offer greater mobility for surgeons and are especially useful in procedures where access to a power source is limited. Pneumatic powered tools are driven by compressed air and are typically used in larger procedures where high power output is necessary. Each type of power tool has its own advantages and is chosen based on the specific needs of the surgery.</p></p>
