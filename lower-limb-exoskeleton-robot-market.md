<p><p>The lower limb exoskeleton robot market is segmented into power-supply and no-power markets based on their operational mechanisms. Power-supply exoskeletons utilize external power sources, such as batteries or electric motors, to enhance mobility and assist users in walking or rehabilitation. In contrast, no-power exoskeletons rely on passive designs, using springs or mechanical elements to provide support without active power, making them lighter and often more affordable. Both types cater to diverse applications, including medical rehabilitation and industrial support.</p></p>
