<p><p>The Rotary Steam Joint market is categorized into two primary types: Self-Supported and Bracket/Rod-Supported joints. Self-Supported joints are designed to rely on their inherent structural integrity to maintain position and function without additional aids. In contrast, Bracket/Rod-Supported joints use external brackets or rods to provide added support and stability, especially in dynamic applications where movement or vibration is present. Each type is tailored for specific operational needs, influencing their performance and application across various industries.</p></p>
