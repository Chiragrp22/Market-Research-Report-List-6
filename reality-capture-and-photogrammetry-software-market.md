Long-distance aerial photography utilizes drones or aircraft to capture large-scale images from high altitudes, ideal for mapping extensive landscapes, urban planning, and agriculture. In contrast, close-range 3D scanning involves detailed capturing of smaller objects or environments using laser scanners or handheld devices, often used in architecture, heritage preservation, and product design for high accuracy and detail. Both markets enable effective data visualization and analysis.</p></p>
