<p><p>Ceramic infrared heat emitters are categorized into medium wave and long wave types based on their emission characteristics. Medium wave emitters operate at temperatures between 1,000 to 1,400 degrees Fahrenheit, providing focused heat that penetrates materials more effectively, making them ideal for industrial applications. Long wave emitters, on the other hand, emit heat at lower temperatures, around 392 to 1,112 degrees Fahrenheit, delivering gentle warmth suitable for heating spaces and applications requiring uniform heat distribution. Both types serve distinct market needs and applications.</p></p>
