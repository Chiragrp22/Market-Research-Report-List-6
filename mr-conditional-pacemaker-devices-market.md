Implantable pacemakers are surgically implanted inside the body, typically in the chest area, and provide continuous pacing support for patients with heart rhythm disorders. Both types of devices are designed to be safe for use in magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) environments, with the necessary precautions and guidelines in place to ensure patient safety.</p></p>
