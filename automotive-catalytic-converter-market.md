<p><p>The automotive catalytic converter market comprises several types, primarily categorized into two-way converters and three-way converters, along with other specialized variants. Two-way converters are designed to reduce carbon monoxide and hydrocarbons, typically used in older vehicles. Three-way converters, more common in modern vehicles, simultaneously reduce nitrogen oxides, hydrocarbons, and carbon monoxide, ensuring stricter emission standards are met. Other market types may include diesel oxidation catalysts and selective catalytic reduction systems, each tailored for specific engine types and emissions control requirements.</p></p>
