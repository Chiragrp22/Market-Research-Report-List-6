<p><p>The dairy concentrate market consists primarily of two types: powder and paste. Dairy powder is produced by removing moisture from liquid milk, resulting in a shelf-stable product that is versatile for various applications in baking, confections, and dairy products. Conversely, dairy paste is a semi-liquid concentrate that retains more moisture and is commonly used in sauces, spreads, and ready-to-eat meals. Both forms offer nutritional benefits and cater to different consumer preferences and industrial needs in the food sector.</p></p>
