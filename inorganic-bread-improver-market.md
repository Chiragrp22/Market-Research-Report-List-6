<p><p>The inorganic bread improver market includes various types that enhance dough quality and bread texture. Emulsifiers improve fat incorporation and dough stability, while enzymes facilitate fermentation and enhance gas retention. Oxidizing agents strengthen gluten structure, contributing to dough elasticity and strength. Reducing agents, in contrast, modify gluten to enhance extensibility, allowing for better shaping. Other additives might include minerals and salts that promote yeast activity or improve shelf life, all contributing to the overall quality of baked products.</p></p>
