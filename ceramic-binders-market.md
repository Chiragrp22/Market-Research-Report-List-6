<p><p>The ceramic binders market is divided into organic and inorganic types, each serving distinct applications. Organic binders, such as polymers, provide flexibility and ease of processing, making them suitable for advanced ceramics and lightweight applications. In contrast, inorganic binders, including clays and phosphates, offer superior thermal stability and strength, ideal for refractory materials and high-temperature applications. Both types play crucial roles in enhancing the performance and durability of ceramic products across various industries, including construction, aerospace, and electronics.</p></p>
