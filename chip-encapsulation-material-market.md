<p><p>The chip encapsulation material market is primarily divided into epoxy-based and non-epoxy-based materials. Epoxy-based materials are widely used due to their excellent adhesion, thermal stability, and moisture resistance, making them ideal for various electronic applications. In contrast, non-epoxy-based materials, which include silicone and polyurethane, offer more flexibility and are suitable for specialized applications requiring unique properties like higher temperature resistance or lower dielectric constants. Both types play a crucial role in protecting semiconductor devices from environmental stressors.</p></p>
