<p><p>Underfloor heating and cooling systems are classified into two main types: electric and hydronic systems. Electric systems utilize electrical cables or mats installed beneath the floor surface to generate heat. These systems are typically easier to install and ideal for smaller areas. In contrast, hydronic systems circulate warm water through pipes installed under the floor, providing uniform heating across larger spaces. Both systems promote energy efficiency and comfort, catering to diverse residential and commercial heating and cooling needs.</p></p>
