<p><p>The automotive composites market utilizes various glass fiber types, each catering to specific performance needs. Sheet Molding Compound (SMC) is ideal for large, complex parts, while Bulk Molding Compound (BMC) is suited for smaller, intricate components. Glass Mat Thermoplastic (GMT) combines ease of molding with strength, while Short Fiber Thermoplastic (SFT) offers quick production at lower costs. Long Fiber Thermoplastic (LFT) enhances durability in challenging applications, and Continuous Fiber Thermoplastic (CFT) provides superior mechanical properties. Phenolic Molding Compound (PMC) is known for its thermal resistance. Other types encompass emerging formulations and hybrid composites.</p></p>
