<p><p>Eye tracking systems are categorized into remote and mobile types based on their application and operation. Remote eye tracking systems use fixed setups, such as cameras or sensors, to capture eye movement from a distance, making them ideal for usability studies and research in controlled environments. In contrast, mobile eye tracking systems are designed for on-the-go use, incorporating lightweight gear to track eye movement in real-world scenarios, such as shopping behaviors or navigation, providing insights into user interactions in dynamic settings.</p></p>
