<p><p>Pediatric extracorporeal blood pumps are devices used in pediatric patients to circulate blood during procedures like cardiopulmonary bypass. Common types of blood pumps include roller pumps, centrifugal pumps, pulsatile pumps, non-occlusive pumps, and others. Roller pumps use rotating rollers to create flow, centrifugal pumps use spinning impellers, pulsatile pumps mimic the natural pulse of the heart, non-occlusive pumps minimize damage to blood cells, and other pumps offer unique mechanisms for blood circulation in pediatric patients. Each type serves specific purposes and has its advantages in different scenarios.</p></p>
