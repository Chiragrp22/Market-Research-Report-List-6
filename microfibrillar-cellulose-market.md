<p><p>The Microfibrillar Cellulose (MFC) market is categorized by production methods, including Acid Hydrolysis, Enzymatic Hydrolysis, and Electrospinning. The Acid Hydrolysis method uses acid to break down cellulose fibers, yielding high purity and fine fibrils. Enzymatic Hydrolysis employs enzymes for a more selective and eco-friendly process, resulting in better properties and applications. Electrospinning, on the other hand, utilizes electric fields to produce nanofibers from MFC, enabling innovative applications in textiles and biomedical fields, emphasizing versatility and advanced material properties.</p></p>
