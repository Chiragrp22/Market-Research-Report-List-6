<p><p>Potassium sulfate (CAS 7778-80-5) is produced through various methods, each influencing market dynamics. The Mannheim process involves reacting potassium chloride with sulfuric acid, yielding potassium sulfate and hydrochloric acid. MOP (Muriate of Potash) is a key source of potassium fertilizer, while the Kieserite process extracts potassium sulfate from magnesium sulfate-rich ores. Lastly, brine processing utilizes salt lakes, where potassium is concentrated through evaporation. Each production type affects supply, pricing, and applications in agriculture and industry.</p></p>
