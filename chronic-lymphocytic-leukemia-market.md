<p><p>The Chronic Lymphocytic Leukemia (CLL) market is part of a broader leukemia landscape, which includes Acute Myeloid Leukemia (AML), Chronic Myeloid Leukemia (CML), and Acute Lymphocytic Leukemia (ALL). CLL primarily affects older adults, characterized by the accumulation of abnormal lymphocytes. In contrast, AML is aggressive and occurs in both adults and children, while CML is marked by the presence of the Philadelphia chromosome and primarily impacts middle-aged individuals. ALL is prevalent in children, featuring immature lymphoblasts. Each type requires distinct treatment approaches and market strategies.</p></p>
